What Are These Calculators?

📈 What is SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a method of investing a fixed amount regularly (monthly or quarterly) in mutual funds. SIP leverages the power of compounding and rupee cost averaging to grow wealth over time. Even ₹1,000/month invested at 12% CAGR for 20 years can grow to over ₹9.9 lakh.

🏦 What is EMI?

An Equated Monthly Instalment (EMI) is the fixed monthly payment you make to repay a loan over its tenure. EMI includes both the principal and the interest component. Our EMI calculator helps you plan home loans, car loans, and personal loans instantly.

🏧 What is FD?

A Fixed Deposit (FD) is a savings instrument offered by banks where you deposit a lump sum for a fixed tenure at a guaranteed interest rate. FDs offer higher returns than savings accounts and are insured by DICGC up to ₹5 lakh. Senior citizens typically get an additional 0.25–0.50% interest rate.

📊 What is CAGR?

Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) measures the mean annual growth rate of an investment over a specified period longer than one year. It represents one of the most accurate ways to calculate and determine returns for anything that can rise or fall in value over time, such as stocks, mutual funds, or real estate.

SIP vs FD vs Mutual Funds – Which is Better?

📈 SIP (Mutual Fund)

  • Returns: 10–14% p.a. (equity mutual funds, historical average)
  • Risk: Market-linked — moderate to high
  • Liquidity: High — redeem anytime (open-ended funds)
  • Tax (LTCG): 12.5% above ₹1.25 lakh/year (FY 2024–25)
  • Minimum: Start from ₹500/month
  • Best for: Long-term wealth creation (5+ years)

🏧 Fixed Deposit (FD)

  • Returns: 6.5–7.5% p.a. (current bank rates)
  • Risk: Zero — guaranteed returns
  • Liquidity: Moderate — premature withdrawal penalty applies
  • Tax: Interest fully taxable as per income slab
  • Minimum: ₹1,000 lump sum
  • Best for: Short-term parking of funds (1–3 years)

🏛️ PPF / NPS

  • Returns: 7.1% p.a. (PPF, government-fixed)
  • Risk: Zero — sovereign guarantee
  • Liquidity: Low — 15-year lock-in (PPF)
  • Tax: EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) for PPF
  • Minimum: ₹500/year (PPF)
  • Best for: Retirement planning with tax benefits

💡 Verdict: For long-term wealth creation (10+ years), SIP in equity mutual funds historically outperforms FD and PPF. For capital protection and short-term goals, FD remains the safest choice. A balanced portfolio often includes all three.
