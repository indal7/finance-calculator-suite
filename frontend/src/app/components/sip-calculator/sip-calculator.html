What is SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a disciplined way to invest in mutual funds. Instead of investing a lump sum, you contribute a fixed amount every month — benefiting from rupee cost averaging and power of compounding over time. SIP is one of the most popular investment methods in India, with over 9 crore active SIP accounts as of 2026.

How SIP Works

Each month, a fixed amount is auto-debited from your bank and invested in mutual fund units at the current NAV (Net Asset Value). When markets are low, you buy more units; when markets are high, you buy fewer — this averages your cost over time. This process is called rupee cost averaging and is one of the key advantages of SIP investing.

SIP Formula – How Returns Are Calculated

The SIP future value is calculated using the compound interest formula:

FV = P × ((1+r)ⁿ − 1) / r × (1+r)

Where P = monthly investment, r = monthly rate (annual rate ÷ 12), n = total months (years × 12).

Real-Life SIP Calculation Examples

Example 1: ₹5,000/month at 12% p.a. for 10 years → Total invested: ₹6,00,000, estimated corpus: ₹11,61,695 (returns of ~94%).

Example 2: ₹1,000/month at 12% p.a. for 20 years → Total invested: ₹2,40,000, estimated corpus: ₹9,99,148 (returns of ~316%).

Example 3: ₹10,000/month at 14% p.a. for 15 years → Total invested: ₹18,00,000, estimated corpus: ₹60,00,000+ — demonstrating the explosive power of compounding over time.

Benefits of SIP Investment in India

  • Start Small: Begin with as little as ₹500/month — no large capital needed.
  • Rupee Cost Averaging: Automatically buys more units when markets fall, reducing average cost.
  • Power of Compounding: Returns earned are reinvested, leading to exponential wealth growth.
  • Disciplined Saving: Auto-debit ensures you invest consistently without emotional decisions.
  • Flexible: Pause, stop, or increase your SIP amount anytime without penalty.
  • Tax Efficiency: ELSS SIP investments qualify for ₹1.5 lakh deduction under Section 80C.
  • Liquidity: Most open-ended equity fund SIPs can be redeemed anytime (unlike FDs).

SIP vs Lump Sum: Which is Better?

SIP is better for investors who receive a regular monthly income and cannot time the market. Lump sum investing is better when you have a large corpus available and markets are at a multi-year low. For most salaried individuals, SIP through equity mutual funds is the most practical and effective wealth-building strategy.

Best SIP Plans in India 2026

Top-performing mutual fund categories for SIP in India include: Flexi-Cap Funds (broad diversification), Large & Mid Cap Funds (balanced risk-reward), Mid Cap Funds (higher growth potential), and ELSS (Tax-Saving) Funds (Section 80C benefit with equity exposure). Always check the fund's 3-year, 5-year and 10-year CAGR before investing.
