What is EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment)?

An Equated Monthly Instalment (EMI) is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender on a specified date each calendar month. EMIs are used to pay off both interest and principal each month so that over a specified number of years, the loan is fully paid off.

EMI Formula – How It Is Calculated

EMI is calculated using the standard reducing balance formula:

EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ / ((1+r)ⁿ − 1)

Where P = principal, r = monthly interest rate (annual ÷ 12), n = total months.

Real-Life EMI Examples

Home Loan: ₹30,00,000 at 8.5% for 20 years → Monthly EMI: ₹26,035, Total Interest: ₹32,48,400.

Car Loan: ₹8,00,000 at 10% for 5 years → Monthly EMI: ₹16,997, Total Interest: ₹2,19,820.

Personal Loan: ₹5,00,000 at 14% for 3 years → Monthly EMI: ₹17,085, Total Interest: ₹1,15,060.

How to Reduce Your EMI

  • Larger Down Payment: Reduces principal, directly cutting EMI and total interest.
  • Negotiate Interest Rate: A 0.5% rate reduction on a ₹30 lakh loan saves over ₹1 lakh in interest.
  • Balance Transfer: Move your loan to a lender offering lower rates to reduce monthly outgo.
  • Longer Tenure: Increases total interest but reduces monthly burden — useful for cash-flow management.
  • Part-Prepayment: Paying extra towards principal reduces outstanding balance and future interest.

EMI for Home Loan vs Personal Loan vs Car Loan

Home loans offer the lowest EMIs (8–10% interest, up to 30-year tenure) and come with significant tax benefits. Car loans are mid-range (9–12%, up to 7 years). Personal loans have the highest EMI burden (11–18%, up to 5 years) but require no collateral. Always compare the total cost of the loan (principal + interest) rather than just the monthly EMI.
