Complete Guide to PPF (Public Provident Fund) 2026

Public Provident Fund (PPF) is a government-backed savings and investment scheme launched by the Ministry of Finance to promote savings among Indian citizens. It combines the safety of government guarantee with reasonable returns (7.1% p.a. as of 2026) and complete tax exemption — making it an ideal investment for conservative investors and those planning for long-term goals like retirement or education.

Key Features of PPF

  • Maturity Period: 15 years (starting from the end of the financial year when you open your account).
  • Extension: After 15 years, you can extend in blocks of 5 years indefinitely.
  • Minimum Deposit: ₹500 per financial year (April to March).
  • Maximum Deposit: ₹1,50,000 per financial year (as per 2026 limits).
  • Interest Rate: 7.1% p.a. (compounded annually) — guaranteed and notified quarterly by the government.
  • Tax Benefit: All returns are 100% tax-free under Section 80C (investment) and Section 10 (returns).
  • Withdrawal Rules: Partial withdrawal from 7th year, full withdrawal after 15 years.
  • Loan Facility: Can take a loan against PPF balance from 7th year onwards.

PPF Contribution Eligibility & Rules

Who can open PPF? Any Indian resident (adults and minors) can open a PPF account. Non-residents cannot open a new account, but existing accounts can be continued if you were a resident when you opened it.

How to contribute? You must contribute at least ₹500 and at most ₹1,50,000 in a financial year (April–March). You don't have to contribute the same amount every year — it can vary, or you can skip a year entirely.

Where to open? PPF accounts can be opened at:

  • Any post office in India
  • Authorized banks (SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis, etc.)
  • Online through banks' net banking portals

PPF Interest Rate History & 2026 Rates

PPF interest rates are announced quarterly by the government and change based on prevailing market conditions. As of Q4 FY 2025-26, the PPF interest rate is 7.1% p.a.

Historical perspective: Rates have ranged from 5.5% to 8.5% in the past decade. Despite market volatility, PPF consistently outperforms bank Fixed Deposits (typically 6–7%) and provides better returns with guaranteed capital protection.

PPF vs Other Investment Options – Comparison

PPF vs FD (Fixed Deposit): PPF offers better tax treatment (100% tax-free returns vs FD interest taxed at income slab rates). For a 15-year investment of ₹50K/year at 7.1% vs FD at 6.5%, PPF delivers ₹1.2L+ more after tax. Plus, PPF combines safety with tax benefits.

PPF vs SIP (Mutual Funds): SIPs typically offer higher returns (10–14% historically) but with market risk. PPF is safer and guaranteed. Use PPF for capital preservation and sure goals; use SIP for wealth creation with market-linked returns.

PPF vs NPS (National Pension Scheme): Both provide tax benefits, but NPS is for retirement planning and has limited withdrawal before age 60. PPF is more flexible — you can withdraw after 7 years, and you receive a lump sum at maturity without monthly pension restrictions.

PPF vs Savings Account: PPF returns (7.1%) far exceed savings account interest (4–5%). ₹1,50,000 invested annually for 15 years grows to ₹41.6L in PPF vs just ₹27L in savings account.

Withdrawal & Loan Rules

Partial Withdrawal (7th year onwards): From the 7th financial year, you can withdraw up to 50% of the balance of the preceding 4 financial years or the balance at the end of the first preceding financial year, whichever is lower.

Full Withdrawal (After 15 years): At maturity, you can withdraw the entire balance (principal + interest). If you don't withdraw, the account is automatically extended in 5-year blocks.

Loan Facility: From the 7th financial year, you can take a loan against your PPF balance. The loan amount is limited to 50% of the balance at the end of the 4 preceding financial years or immediately preceding year.

Tax Benefits of PPF

  • Section 80C Deduction: Your annual PPF investment (up to ₹1,50,000) is deductible from your taxable income.
  • Tax-Free Returns: All interest earned is completely tax-free under Section 10 of the Income Tax Act.
  • Tax-Free Maturity: The final amount received at maturity is 100% tax-free.
  • No TDS: Unlike FDs where TDS is deducted on interest, PPF interest has no TDS.

Real Example: For a person in the 30% tax bracket investing ₹1,50,000/year:

  • Tax savings from Section 80C deduction: ₹45,000/year (₹1,50,000 × 30%)
  • Interest earned: ₹1,00,000+ (over 15 years, all tax-free)
  • Total tax benefit: ₹10,00,000+ over 15 years

Best Practices for PPF Investment

  • Start Early: The earlier you start, the more time your money has to compound. Starting at age 25 vs 35 can mean ₹5L+ difference.
  • Invest Regularly: Contribute consistently every financial year to maximize compounding benefits.
  • Maximum Contribution: If possible, invest the maximum ₹1,50,000/year to utilize your full Section 80C limit.
  • Combine with Other Tools: Use PPF for safety and tax savings; combine with SIP for growth.
  • Extend After 15 Years: At maturity, you can extend in 5-year blocks to continue enjoying returns and tax benefits.
