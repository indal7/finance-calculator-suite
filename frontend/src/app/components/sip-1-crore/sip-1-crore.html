Building a corpus of ₹1 crore (₹1,00,00,000) through SIP is one of the most searched financial goals in India. The good news? It's entirely achievable through disciplined monthly investing in equity mutual funds — even with moderate monthly contributions.

Three Strategies to Reach ₹1 Crore Faster

  • Start early: Starting at age 25 gives you 30+ years of compounding. ₹2,800/month at 12% for 30 years = ₹1 crore with only ₹10 lakh invested.
  • Use step-up SIP: Increasing SIP by 10% annually (matching salary hikes) dramatically cuts the timeline. ₹5,000 starting SIP with 10% step-up reaches ₹1 crore in ~16 years vs ~24 years with flat SIP.
  • Choose high-growth funds: Mid-cap and small-cap funds have delivered 15–18% CAGR over 10+ year periods. Higher returns mean lower monthly SIP requirements.

₹1 Crore SIP Plan for Different Life Stages

  • Age 22–25 (Fresh graduate): Start with ₹3,000–₹5,000/month in flexi-cap or index fund. Step-up 10% annually. You have 30+ years — even low SIP amounts compound dramatically.
  • Age 30–35 (Mid-career): ₹15,000–₹20,000/month in diversified equity funds. 15–20 year horizon is ideal. Step-up SIP strongly recommended.
  • Age 40+ (Senior professional): ₹30,000–₹50,000/month with a mix of large-cap and balanced funds. Shorter horizon requires higher contributions but lower volatility tolerance.

The Power of Compounding — Why ₹1 Crore is Achievable

At 12% return with ₹10,000/month SIP for 20 years: you invest ₹24 lakh total, but your corpus grows to approximately ₹1 crore. That means ₹76 lakh comes from compounding — over 3× your own contribution. The longer you stay invested, the larger the compounding multiplier becomes.
